# Healthcare Compliance — UAE

## Regulatory Framework

### Regulatory Bodies by Emirate

| Emirate | Authority | Portal | Facility Categories |
|---------|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| Dubai | DHA (Dubai Health Authority) | Sheryan | Hospital, Outpatient, Diagnostic, Pharmacy, Home Care |
| Abu Dhabi/Al Ain | DOH (Department of Health) | TAMM/DOH eLicensing | Same + additional categories |
| Sharjah, Ajman, UAQ, RAK, Fujairah | MOHAP (Ministry of Health and Prevention) | Sharik | Federal categories |

### Key Healthcare Legislation

| Law | Citation | Scope |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Private Health Facilities | Federal Law No. 4 of 2015 (as amended by Federal Law No. 8 of 2023) | Facility licensing, operations |
| Medical Liability | Federal Law No. 4 of 2016 | Malpractice, informed consent |
| Mental Health | Federal Law No. 10 of 2023 | Mental health services, admissions |
| Medical Products | Federal Decree-Law No. 38 of 2024 | Pharmaceuticals, medical devices |
| Health Insurance (Dubai) | Law No. 11 of 2013 | Mandatory health insurance |

## DHA Facility Categories (Manual for Licensing Health Facilities v1.1, Nov 2024)

### Outpatient Facilities

| Category | Description | Key Requirements |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **General Clinic** | Single GP or multiple GPs | Min 30 sqm per consultation room |
| **Specialty Clinic** | Single specialty (e.g., dermatology) | Specialist + support staff |
| **Polyclinic** | Multiple specialties | Medical Director required |
| **Day Surgery Center** | Outpatient surgical procedures | Full surgical setup, recovery area |
| **Express Clinic** | Clinic within pharmacy | Family Medicine/Internal Medicine only |
| **Hotel Clinic** | In-house hotel clinic | Limited services |

### Inpatient Facilities

| Category | Description | Key Requirements |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **General Hospital** | Multi-specialty inpatient care | Min bed count, 24/7 emergency |
| **Specialty Hospital** | Single specialty (e.g., cardiac) | Specialty-specific standards |
| **Rehabilitation Center** | Physical/occupational therapy | Therapy rooms, equipment |

### Support Facilities

| Category | Description | Key Requirements |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Diagnostic Center** | Lab and/or radiology | NABIDH connectivity required |
| **Pharmacy** | Retail or hospital pharmacy | Pharmacist-in-charge |
| **Home Healthcare Provider** | Services at patient's home | See below |

## Home Healthcare Licensing

### DHA Home Healthcare Provider Requirements

```
FACILITY REQUIREMENTS:
- Registered office in Dubai (not required to be clinical space)
- NABIDH connectivity for patient records
- Medical Director (licensed physician, min 2 years UAE experience)
- Quality management system
- Medical waste disposal contract

PROFESSIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- All clinical staff must hold valid DHA licenses
- Nurses: RN or above for clinical services
- Caregivers: Must be trained, supervised by RN
- Physicians: Required for prescribing, certain procedures

SCOPE OF SERVICES (Typical):
- Nursing care (wound care, injections, IV therapy)
- Physiotherapy
- Caregiver/companion services
- Lab sample collection
- Vital signs monitoring
- Medication administration (per physician order)

PROHIBITED WITHOUT ADDITIONAL LICENSING:
- Surgical procedures
- Anesthesia
- Controlled substance administration (requires specific protocols)
- Diagnostic imaging
```

### DOH (Abu Dhabi) Home Healthcare

```
KEY DIFFERENCES FROM DHA:
- Apply through TAMM portal
- DOH-specific facility standards
- Separate professional licensing (DOH license required)
- Different fee structure

EXPANSION FROM DUBAI TO ABU DHABI:
1. Obtain separate DOH facility license
2. Transfer or obtain DOH licenses for all staff
3. NABIDH equivalent: Malaffi (Abu Dhabi HIE)
4. Separate medical waste contracts
5. Separate insurance requirements
```

## Professional Licensing (PQR v3, 2024)

### Unified PQR Framework

```
APPLICABLE TO: DHA, DOH, MOHAP, SHA

GENERAL REQUIREMENTS (All Professions):
1. Recognized qualification (per authority's approved list)
2. Valid home country license/registration
3. Good Standing Certificate (GSC) - max 6 months old
4. DataFlow Primary Source Verification (PSV)
5. No practice gap > 2 years
6. Pass examination (unless exempt)

EXAMINATION REQUIREMENTS:
- Computer-Based Test (CBT): Prometric or Pearson VUE
- Oral Assessment: May be required for certain categories
- Maximum 3 attempts per authority
- Can attempt with different authority after 3 failures

TIER CLASSIFICATION (affects exam exemption):
- Tier 1: Recognized countries (exam exemption possible)
- Tier 2: Conditionally recognized (exam usually required)
- Tier 3: Additional assessment required
```

### Physician Licensing

```
REQUIREMENTS:
- MBBS/MD from recognized institution
- Specialty qualification (for specialist/consultant)
- Min 2 years post-qualification experience (specialist)
- Min 5 years post-specialty experience (consultant)
- Valid license from country of last practice
- DataFlow verification
- CBT exam (unless Tier 1 exempt)
- Oral assessment (for consultant level)

TITLES:
- General Practitioner (GP)
- Specialist
- Consultant

FEES (DHA, approximate):
- Registration: AED 1,000 - 2,000
- License activation: AED 2,000 - 5,000
- Exam: AED 1,000 - 1,500
```

### Nurse Licensing

```
REQUIREMENTS:
- BSN/Nursing Degree from recognized institution
- Min 2 years post-qualification experience
- Valid license from country of last practice
- DataFlow verification
- CBT exam

TITLES:
- Registered Nurse (RN) - requires degree
- Practical Nurse (PN) / Assistant Nurse - diploma acceptable

SUPERVISION REQUIREMENTS:
- PN must work under RN supervision
- Ratios vary by facility type and authority
```

## NABIDH Compliance (Dubai)

```
WHAT IS NABIDH?
National Backbone for Integrated Dubai Health
- Mandatory health information exchange
- All licensed facilities must connect
- Standardized coding (ICD-10-CM, CPT)

REQUIREMENTS:
1. EMR system with NABIDH integration capability
2. Signed NABIDH contract with DHA
3. Successful connectivity testing
4. Ongoing data submission compliance

NON-COMPLIANCE PENALTIES:
- License suspension
- Fines
- Inability to process insurance claims

TIMELINE:
- Must be connected before facility license activation
- Existing facilities had transition period (now expired)
```

## Healthcare Advertising Restrictions

```
DHA MARKETING GUIDELINES:

PROHIBITED:
- Unsubstantiated claims ("best", "guaranteed results")
- Before/after photos without proper consent
- Pricing that could mislead
- Comparisons with other providers
- Celebrity endorsements (without approval)
- Promotion of unlicensed services

REQUIRED:
- DHA license number on all materials
- Accurate representation of services
- Proper disclaimers
- Approval for certain campaign types

SOCIAL MEDIA:
- Same rules apply
- Must comply with UAE Cybercrime Law
- Influencer partnerships require disclosure
- Cannot promote prescription medications to public

PENALTIES:
- Warning
- Fine (up to AED 100,000)
- License suspension
- License revocation (repeat offenders)
```

## Facility Licensing Timeline (Typical)

```
DHA NEW CLINIC:

Week 1-2: Trade license application (DET)
Week 2-4: Initial approval submission (Sheryan)
Week 4-6: Layout/engineering review
Week 6-8: Staff licensing (if not already licensed)
Week 8-10: NABIDH connectivity
Week 10-12: Inspection
Week 12-14: License issuance

TOTAL: 3-4 months (if no delays)

COMMON DELAYS:
- Layout non-compliance (add 2-4 weeks)
- Staff licensing issues (add 4-8 weeks)
- NABIDH integration problems (add 2-4 weeks)
```
